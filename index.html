Is full self-driving feasible to achieve?

Self-driving cars are autonomous decision-making systems that make informed decisions using data collected from different onboard resources like GPS, Cameras, Radars, Lidars, and Ultrasonic Sensors. The last decade witnessed increasingly rapid progress in self-driving vehicle technology, mainly backed up by advances in deep learning and artificial intelligence. The term self-driving cars are relatively self-explanatory, but five SAE Levels are used to define autonomous driving. Lower-level cars require essential driver assistance, while higher SAE levels involve no human interaction whatsoever. Cars at level 5 will have no foot pedals and steering wheels. While it might be feasible to make an entirely self-driving car, I can undoubtedly say that we are decades away from making a fully self-driving car unless we have some significant breakthroughs in the field of Artificial Intelligence. Even though many predictions were made about the launch of self-driving cars, none of it has been fulfilled.
The first problem manufacturers of self-driving cars face is object detection and localization of the vehicles. While considering the problem of object detection and scene detection, the de-facto standardization is the convoluted neural network. Convolutional neural networks (CNN) are used to model spatial information, such as images. For example, CNN is used to detect traffic signs, signboards and classify between images. However, a challenge in detecting signboards is doing it quickly and accurately. The CNN can be trained with a lot of datasets. However, it isn't very easy to generalize based on the dataset. The signboard can be dusty, covered with leaves, twisted in an odd angle, or the signboard may be of a different country, and the CNN might not have been trained on that dataset. It's also typical to alter or put up temporary road signs, such as those alerting of a detour or road construction. As a result, CNN must be quick to analyze information and identify warning indicators.
The second problem Faced is pathfinding and autonomous navigation. The pathfinding approaches like A star algorithm or hybrid A star algorithms that are in the lines of Repeated A star algorithm can be employed for the pathfinding algorithms. Although there are many much better and complex algorithms than the simple A star algorithm, all of them fail to access the dynamic nature of the environment as these algorithms tend to give paths in straight lines, which is not always the case in the real world.
The final problem self-driving cars face is making complex decisions like stopping when seeing a pedestrian or stopping when another car is about to cross. Markov decision processes and reinforcement learning is the way to go in such cases, the self-driving car senses the environment with an observation I, performs an action a. in state s, interacts with its environment through a received reward R, and transits to the next state s following a transition function Ts. Stopping for a pedestrian is a reasonably simple problem, and Reinforcement Learning can handle such problems. However, it is difficult for self-driving cars to solve the trolley problem, which states that in case of brake failure will the car kill five people or one person, such problems are difficult to handle, and a lot of research is done in this line, but no significant process is made.
Almost the whole industry predicted that self-driving vehicles would be on our roads by now, only to admit that they misjudged how difficult it was to get automobiles to drive themselves safely and consistently. Self-driving cars may be the way of the future, but there are still some significant roadblocks in the way. And without a significant breakthrough in the field of Artificial intelligence, we are decades away from a fully automated self-driving car.

Can AIs be creative?Can humans?What is creativity?

Before answering whether AI is creative or not, I want to define creativity. Creativity can be defined as doing something or making something that has never been seen before or is completely random and truly original action or a piece of art. From the above definition of "creativity," we can infer that it is relatively subjective, as something unique to one person might not be unusual for another person. To address whether AI can be creative? recent advancements in AI suggest that the answer is a "yes."
To begin with, no human is born with the ability to write poems, compose music or draw paintings; we learn it from experience and over time. We call this process learning. Don't you think AI does the same things as observing and analyzing data and learning creative skills like poem writing, composing a song, or drawing a painting depending on the dataset provided? If we feed an AI with a million paintings, it can surely come up with its original artwork that was created before, which sums up the definition of creativity. There have been several examples where AI has proved itself creative. One of them is when AARON(an algorithm) made a painting called Starting over. AI-generated poetry can now fool humans into thinking a human has written it.
When thinking about AI being creative, the number one AI tool that comes to my mind is GANS or Generative Adversarial Networks. A GAN is a generative model that uses two neural network models to train it. The "generator" or "generative network" model learns to produce fresh believable examples. The "discriminator" or "discriminative network" model, on the other hand, learns to distinguish produced from actual samples. The two models are set up in a game theory model. The generator model attempts to deceive the discriminator model, and the discriminator is given both actual and produced sample cases. So here, we can see that in a sense, GANS is designed to be creative as the discriminator won't allow the generator to make an output that is already present in the dataset. So, if we have a vast dataset of cat images, we can train the GANs to create an image of the cat not in the dataset. A creative example of this is that GANs can color black and white photos that are uniques and never seen before. It can be used to reconstruct photographs of faces with specific specified features, such as changes in hair color, style, facial expression, and even gender.
AI development hit a milestone when AlphaGO beat a Go champion to conquer the game. When AlphaGo beat the champion, many commentators commented that the moves were like never seen before as the game of Go is simple. Still, the strategies involved are complex. So AlphaGo used never seen moves which led it to conquer the game. The architecture of alpha has a convolutional and dense layer, which allows it to make complex moves that I believe are creative.
To conclude, I would say that even though the term "creativity" is subjective , AI can be creative as they possess the ability to create new and random things. The argument against AI being creative is that an algorithm can only accomplish what it is designed to do. Hence it can't possibly do anything new, and the programmer is creative, not the machine.
